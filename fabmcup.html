    Topic: Introduction to Accounting
    What is often referred to as the language of business?
    • A) Economics
    • B) Accounting
    • C) Management
    • D) Marketing

    Explanation: Introduction to Accounting

    Correct Answer: B

    Accounting is universally known as the "language of business" because it is the primary method used to communicate financial information to various stakeholders.

    Topic: Branches of Accounting
    Which branch of accounting primarily deals with the preparation of general-purpose financial statements for external users?
    • A) Management Accounting
    • B) Tax Accounting
    • C) Financial Accounting
    • D) Cost Accounting

    Explanation: Branches of Accounting

    Correct Answer: C

    Financial Accounting focuses on recording, summarizing, and reporting the myriad of transactions resulting from business operations over a period of time for the benefit of external users like investors and creditors.

    Topic: Users of Accounting Information
    Who among the following is considered an internal user of accounting information?
    • A) Potential Investors
    • B) Bank Creditors
    • C) Government Regulators
    • D) Company Managers

    Explanation: Users of Accounting Information

    Correct Answer: D

    Company managers are internal users because they are directly involved in managing and operating the business. Investors, creditors, and the government are external users.

    Topic: Forms of Business Organizations
    What type of business organization is owned by only one person who has unlimited liability?
    • A) Corporation
    • B) Partnership
    • C) Sole Proprietorship
    • D) Cooperative

    Explanation: Forms of Business Organizations

    Correct Answer: C

    A sole proprietorship is the simplest form of business, owned and run by one individual who takes all the profits but also assumes unlimited liability for all business debts.

    Topic: Accounting Concepts and Principles
    Which accounting principle assumes that a business will continue to operate indefinitely into the foreseeable future?
    • A) Going Concern Principle
    • B) Business Entity Principle
    • C) Periodicity Principle
    • D) Objectivity Principle

    Explanation: Accounting Concepts and Principles

    Correct Answer: A

    The Going Concern Principle assumes the business will remain in operation long enough to carry out its existing objectives and commitments, justifying the deferral of certain expenses.

    Topic: The Accounting Equation
    Which of the following represents the correct and fundamental accounting equation?
    • A) Assets = Liabilities - Equity
    • B) Assets = Liabilities + Equity
    • C) Liabilities = Assets + Equity
    • D) Equity = Assets + Liabilities

    Explanation: The Accounting Equation

    Correct Answer: B

    The fundamental accounting equation states that everything a business owns (Assets) is financed either by borrowing money (Liabilities) or by the owners' investments (Equity).

    Topic: Books of Accounts
    Which book of accounts is traditionally known as the "book of original entry"?
    • A) General Ledger
    • B) Subsidiary Ledger
    • C) General Journal
    • D) Trial Balance

    Explanation: Books of Accounts

    Correct Answer: C

    The General Journal is called the book of original entry because it is the first place where business transactions are recorded in chronological order before they are posted to the ledger.

    Topic: Books of Accounts
    A company purchases new equipment for ₱10,000 in cash. How is this recorded in the General Journal?
    • A) Increase Equipment, Increase Cash
    • B) Increase Equipment, Decrease Cash
    • C) Decrease Equipment, Increase Cash
    • D) Decrease Equipment, Decrease Cash

    Explanation: Books of Accounts

    Correct Answer: B

    When you buy equipment for cash, you are acquiring an asset (Equipment goes up) and giving up an asset (Cash goes down) simultaneously.

    Topic: Accounting Cycle (Service)
    If a service business has total assets amounting to ₱50,000 and total equity of ₱30,000, what is the total amount of its liabilities?
    • A) ₱80,000
    • B) ₱20,000
    • C) ₱50,000
    • D) ₱30,000

    Explanation: Accounting Cycle (Service)

    Correct Answer: B

    Using the accounting equation Assets = Liabilities + Equity, we substitute the given values: ₱50,000 = Liabilities + ₱30,000. Therefore, Liabilities = ₱50,000 - ₱30,000 = ₱20,000.

    Topic: Accounting Concepts and Principles
    Which principle states that the personal expenses of an owner must be kept entirely separate from the expenses of their business?
    • A) Periodicity Principle
    • B) Going Concern Principle
    • C) Objective Evidence Principle
    • D) Business Entity Principle

    Explanation: Accounting Concepts and Principles

    Correct Answer: D

    The Business Entity Principle assumes that a business is a separate and distinct legal entity from its owners. Therefore, personal transactions of the owner should not be recorded in the business's books.

    Topic: The Accounting Equation and Major Accounts
    Which of the following is classified as an Asset account?
    • A) Accounts Payable
    • B) Owner's Capital
    • C) Cash
    • D) Service Revenue

    Explanation: The Accounting Equation and Major Accounts

    Correct Answer: C

    Cash is a present economic resource controlled by the entity as a result of past events, making it an asset. Accounts Payable is a liability, Owner's Capital is equity, and Service Revenue is an income account.

    Topic: Books of Accounts
    The General Ledger is commonly referred to as the:
    • A) Book of Original Entry
    • B) Book of Final Entry
    • C) Source Document
    • D) Trial Balance

    Explanation: Books of Accounts

    Correct Answer: B

    The General Ledger is known as the "book of final entry" because it is the destination where all transactions recorded in the journals (the books of original entry) are eventually posted and summarized by account.

    Topic: Books of Accounts
    A business collected ₱15,000 in cash from a customer for services already rendered. How is this recorded in the books?
    • A) Increase Cash, Increase Accounts Receivable
    • B) Decrease Cash, Increase Service Revenue
    • C) Increase Cash, Increase Service Revenue
    • D) Increase Cash, Decrease Accounts Payable

    Explanation: Books of Accounts

    Correct Answer: C

    Receiving cash for a service provided immediately increases the company's Cash (an asset) and also increases its Service Revenue (an income account).

    Topic: Accounting Cycle (Service)
    A service business earned ₱40,000 in revenues and incurred ₱25,000 in expenses for the month. What is the net income or net loss?
    • A) ₱15,000 Net Loss
    • B) ₱15,000 Net Income
    • C) ₱65,000 Net Income
    • D) ₱40,000 Net Income

    Explanation: Accounting Cycle (Service)

    Correct Answer: B

    Net Income is calculated by subtracting Total Expenses from Total Revenues. Since Revenues (₱40,000) are greater than Expenses (₱25,000), the business has a Net Income of ₱15,000.

    Topic: Share Capital Issuance
    A corporation issues 10,000 shares of P100 par value ordinary shares for P150 per share. What is the amount to be credited to the Share Premium account?

    Explanation: Share Capital Issuance

    Correct Answer: 500,000

    The total cash received is P1,500,000 (10,000 shares * P150). The ordinary share capital account is credited for the par value: P1,000,000 (10,000 shares * P100). The excess over par is credited to share premium: P1,500,000 - P1,000,000 = P500,000.

    Topic: Treasury Shares
    When a corporation acquires its own shares, how is this transaction recorded under the cost method?
    • A) As an asset
    • B) As a deduction from total shareholders' equity
    • C) As a deduction from retained earnings only
    • D) As an increase in share premium

    Explanation: Treasury Shares

    Correct Answer: B

    Treasury shares are not assets. Under the cost method, the cost of the acquired shares is recorded in a contra-equity account, which reduces the total shareholders' equity on the statement of financial position.
