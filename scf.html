1. Overview and Purpose

Definition

The statement of cash flows summarizes cash and cash equivalents entering and leaving a company. It strips away non-cash transactions to reveal pure liquidity over a specific period.

Purpose & Role

  • Assesses the entity's ability to generate cash to pay obligations.
  • Reconciles accrual-based Net Income to actual cash flow.
  • Evaluates the financial structure (debt vs. equity).
  • Predicts future cash flows based on historical performance.

2. Cash and Cash Equivalents

The PAS 7 Criteria

  1. Short-term and highly liquid.
  2. Readily convertible to known amounts of cash.
  3. Subject to an insignificant risk of changes in value.

Core Rule: To qualify, an investment must have a maturity of three months or less from its acquisition date.

Examples

  • 30-Day Treasury Bill: YES - Short-term, highly liquid.
  • Investment in Equity: NO - High risk of value change.
  • 1-Year Time Deposit: NO - Maturity > 3 months.

3. Classification of Cash Flows

Operating Activities

Principal revenue-producing activities.

  • (+) Inflows: Sale of goods, royalties, fees.
  • (-) Outflows: Payments to suppliers, employees.

Investing Activities

Acquisition and disposal of long-term assets.

  • (+) Inflows: Sale of PPE, debt/equity instruments.
  • (-) Outflows: Purchase of PPE, loans made.

Financing Activities

Changes in size/composition of equity and borrowings.

  • (+) Inflows: Proceeds from issuing shares, loans.
  • (-) Outflows: Loan repayments, treasury stock, dividends.

4. Special Treatments (PAS 7)

Bank Overdrafts

Generally financing activities. In the PH, they are not permitted to be cash equivalents and are strictly current liability/financing.

Interest and Dividends

  • Interest Paid: Operating/Financing
  • Interest Received: Operating/Investing
  • Dividends Paid: Financing/Operating
  • Dividends Received: Operating/Investing

Non-Cash Transactions

Excluded from the body of the CF statement. Disclosed in notes (e.g., converting bonds to shares).

5. Reporting Methods: Indirect

Adjusts accrual Net Income for non-cash effects, deferrals, and accruals.

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Start with Net Income: From the Income Statement.
  2. Add back Non-Cash Exp: e.g., Depreciation.
  3. Adjust Non-Operating Gains/Losses: Deduct gains, add losses from asset sales to avoid double counting.
  4. Adjust Working Capital:
    • Current Assets: Inc = Deduct, Dec = Add.
    • Current Liab: Inc = Add, Dec = Deduct.

6. Reporting Methods: Direct

The Direct Method explicitly shows major classes of gross cash receipts and payments. It provides more transparent insights than the indirect method.

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Cash from Customers:
    Sales +/- Dec/Inc in AR
  2. Cash to Suppliers:
    COGS +/- Inc/Dec Inventory +/- Dec/Inc AP
  3. Cash for OpEx:
    OpEx (no non-cash) +/- Inc/Dec Prepayments/Accruals
  4. Interest and Taxes:
    Deduct cash directly paid.
