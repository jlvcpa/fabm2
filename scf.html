Overview and Purpose Definition The statement of cash flows summarizes cash and cash equivalents entering and leaving a company. It strips away non-cash transactions to reveal pure liquidity over a specific period. Purpose & Role Assesses the entity's ability to generate cash to pay obligations. Reconciles accrual-based Net Income to actual cash flow. Evaluates the financial structure (debt vs. equity). Predicts future cash flows based on historical performance. 2. Cash and Cash Equivalents The PAS 7 Criteria Short-term and highly liquid. Readily convertible to known amounts of cash. Subject to an insignificant risk of changes in value. Core Rule: To qualify, an investment must have a maturity of three months or less from its acquisition date. Examples 30-Day Treasury Bill: YES - Short-term, highly liquid. Investment in Equity: NO - High risk of value change. 1-Year Time Deposit: NO - Maturity > 3 months. 3. Classification of Cash Flows Operating Activities Principal revenue-producing activities. (+) Inflows: Sale of goods, royalties, fees. (-) Outflows: Payments to suppliers, employees. Investing Activities Acquisition and disposal of long-term assets. (+) Inflows: Sale of PPE, debt/equity instruments. (-) Outflows: Purchase of PPE, loans made. Financing Activities Changes in size/composition of equity and borrowings. (+) Inflows: Proceeds from issuing shares, loans. (-) Outflows: Loan repayments, treasury stock, dividends. 4. Special Treatments (PAS 7) Bank Overdrafts Generally financing activities. In the PH, they are not permitted to be cash equivalents and are strictly current liability/financing. Interest and Dividends Interest Paid: Operating/Financing Interest Received: Operating/Investing Dividends Paid: Financing/Operating Dividends Received: Operating/Investing Non-Cash Transactions Excluded from the body of the CF statement. Disclosed in notes (e.g., converting bonds to shares). 5. Reporting Methods: Indirect Adjusts accrual Net Income for non-cash effects, deferrals, and accruals. Step-by-Step Procedure Start with Net Income: From the Income Statement. Add back Non-Cash Exp: e.g., Depreciation. Adjust Non-Operating Gains/Losses: Deduct gains, add losses from asset sales to avoid double counting. Adjust Working Capital: Current Assets: Inc = Deduct, Dec = Add.
