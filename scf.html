Statement of Cash Flows (PAS 7)

Welcome. Mastering the translation of accrual-based accounting into pure cash reality is paramount. This interactive module guides you through PAS 7.

1. Overview and Purpose

Definition

Summarizes cash and cash equivalents entering and leaving. Strips away non-cash transactions to reveal pure liquidity over a specific period.

Purpose & Role

  • Assesses ability to generate cash to pay obligations.
  • Reconciles accrual Net Income to cash.
  • Evaluates financial structure (debt vs equity).
  • Predicts future cash flows.

2. Cash and Cash Equivalents

The PAS 7 Criteria

  1. Short-term and highly liquid.
  2. Readily convertible to known amounts of cash.
  3. Insignificant risk of changes in value.
  4. Rule: Maturity of three months or less from acquisition date.

3. Classification of Cash Flows

Principal revenue-producing activities. Generally result from transactions determining net income.

+ Inflows

  • Sale of goods/services.
  • Royalties, fees.
  • Trading securities sales.

− Outflows

  • Payments to suppliers/employees.
  • Trading securities purchases.

Acquisition/disposal of long-term assets and non-cash equivalent investments.

+ Inflows

  • Sale of PPE.
  • Sale of debt/equity instruments.

− Outflows

  • Purchase of PPE.
  • Loans made to other parties.

Changes in size/composition of contributed equity and borrowings (non-current liabilities & equity).

+ Inflows

  • Proceeds from issuing shares.
  • Proceeds from loans/bonds.

− Outflows

  • Repayments of loans.
  • Acquiring treasury stock.
  • Dividends paid.

4. Special Treatments (PAS 7)

Generally financing. But if repayable on demand & part of cash management, it's a cash equivalent. In the PH, generally not permitted (strictly current liability/financing).
Int. Paid (Operating/Financing), Int. Received (Operating/Investing), Div. Paid (Financing/Operating), Div. Received (Operating/Investing). Must be consistent.
Excluded from the body of the CF statement. Disclosed in notes (e.g., converting bonds to shares, acquiring assets via lease).

5. Reporting Methods (Operating)

The Direct Method

Shows major classes of gross receipts/payments (e.g., Cash from customers). Recommended by PAS 7.

The Indirect Method

Adjusts accrual Net Income for non-cash effects, deferrals, and accruals. Widely used in practice.
